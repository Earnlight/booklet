If you accept an existence without light, you will be diseased. This is a guarantee. This is because darkness is infectious, sticky and poisonous—it will consume you alive if there is no appropriate defence in place to prevent its doing so. If you strive for, with and to light, you will combat disease through light’s usage if its presence is ever near you and your body will actively fight against disease without you needing to lift a finger. A by-product of having high levels of light.

Striving for something, whatever it may be, without light, will result in disappointment in your levels of satisfaction when that thing is achieved. To explain, you may not feel any different after having put in much work to achieve something and so you feel disappointment that it didn’t satisfy you as you thought it would have. This is the case 100% of the time. Considering personal satisfaction ranks higher than reputation when the general life of a person is considered, striving to appear a certain way without light will leave the person in severe detriment, even if their intentions appear to be good—they cannot in reality be good because they have opted to ignore light and strive into predatory and evil existences. Striving with light ensures your endgame is correct and results in satisfaction along the entire way, especially at the end, and in the future, where you will revisit your success privately or publicly and feel pride and happiness at your efforts and most importantly, your existence.

Light is the cure to delusion. Knowledge defeats delusion in battles, but light is its cure, ending the war. It replaces delusion with living and being in a real state of existence. Light destroys all opposition against it by default, and so it destroys delusion which seeks to eliminate even the potential for light. Remembering your light or striving with your light are ways to combat delusion and remove its effectiveness from yourself.

Light is the only thing that will never be taken from you. Everything else will, eventually. Light is the only thing that we will carry with us from this world on the day of resurrection and beyond, so strive for its accumulation simply by intending to do so and watching as your life falls into place to support you in your quest to do so. Life revolves around intent—intend to be a proponent of light and you will be one, regardless of levels of success (commitment and acting when required are factors of levels of success), but remember—the greater your status, the greater your satisfaction and the purer your intention and being, the greater your enjoyment.

Light comes from truth and its following/implementation. The acknowledgement, enactment and following of truth all result in light. Light carries the evidence of that which has happened, especially in a persons life. The person that is a successful carrier of light is a person that has been through much struggle and hardship, only to come out of the end of the tunnel shining with the light of one who has a greater understanding than others and a greater fortitude, characteristics that are vastly desirable to those who possess reason, and only obtainable through intense hardship.

Light also (almost exclusively) comes from dignified responses. If, for some reason, you suffer, and respond with dignity, your light will increase and your satisfaction from it will remain unhindered, in the sense that you will have no regrets for if you had acted as less than someone who had the capability and wherewithal to respond appropriately but chose not to. Light is the evidence of pure, good and honourable actions in a persons life, on a persons face and in their mannerisms, and it is what separates all beings from each other.

Light elicits the best responses from people and satisfies completely. Light encompasses truth, for without light, truth cannot be seen. Light facilitates continuity, and truth is its forceful brother, enacting change firmly instead of gently. Truth also requires purity to be accessed, which is directly related to light but difficult to grasp for someone who hasn’t spent much time striving to understand this reality. Most choose to idle, rather than strive due to the wide availability of distractions that they can participate in. When the day of resurrection arrives, much regret will be had for the persistent indulging in distractions over striving for a pure evolution, but that is a problem only for the perpetrators of it. We ourselves must strive within our boundaries and within our lane—there is no reason for transgressions when a person remembers and utilises their light—and The Penalties for transgressing while in a higher state of consciousness are more severe than they would be for somebody who already is at a lower level of consciousness and station of life. Being at higher stations results in productivity, potential and actualisations of a better quality and higher frequency—would you not then desire to live a life instead of living day-to-day?

The Noegenesis of Lucius Earnlight

Not all cultures place the same high value on writing as we do today.
In his dialogue with Phaedrus, Socrates criticizes the invention of writing and emphasizes the importance of the spoken word:

Platon’s Works III
 
Chapter 8
Socrates, in Dialogue with Phaedrus

Sokrates:
 
So I have heard that at Naucrates in Egypt there was one of the ancient gods, to whom also the bird called Ibis was sacred, but he himself, the god, was called Theuth. He is said to have invented number and calculation, furthermore, the art of geometry and astronomy, and also the game at draugts and dice, and likewise the letters.
At that time Thamus was king of all Egypt, ruled in the large city of the upper country, which the Hellenes call the Egyptian Thebe, however the god himself was called Ammon.

To him Theuth is said to have gone, to show him his arts, and to ask that they be communicated to the other Egyptians. The former asked of what use each of them arts would be, and depending on what Theuth said about it, he either blamed or praised. Thamus is said to have replied many things to Theuth for and against about each art, which would be too extensive to be mentioned here. But when they came to the letters, Theuth said:
This art, O king, will make the Egyptians wiser and richer in memory, for it has been invented as a means for the understanding and the memory.

But the other replied:

O ingenious Theuth, one knows how to bring to life what belongs to the arts; another to judge how much harm or advantage they might bring to those who make use of them. Thus thou, too, as the father of the letters, hast said out of love for them, the opposite of what they will actually bring. For this invention will rather instil forgetfulness in the souls of the learners out of neglect of the memory, because, relying on these letters, they will remember rather from the outside by means of signs foreign to the matter, but not within them and directly.

Not, therefore, for the memory, but only for recollection, thou hast invented a means; and of wisdom thou teach thy apprentices only the appearance, not the thing itself.

For having heard many things without instruction, they will also believe themselves to be knowing, though they are yet ignorant for the most part and difficult to be treated, after they have become conceited instead of wise.

[...]
 

For writing, Phaedrus, has this bad thing, and in this it is actually quite similar to painting; for this also puts down its excerpts as living, but if one asks them something, then they are quite reverently still. The same is true of the writings. You might think that they speak as if they understood something, but if you ask them eagerly about what they say, they always contain only one and the same thing.

But once it is written, every speech roams among those who understand it, and among those to whom it does not belong, and it does not understand to whom it shall speak, and to whom it shall not. And if she is offended or undeservedly insulted, she always needs her father’s help; for she alone is not able to help or protect herself.
